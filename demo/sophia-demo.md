# The Sophia Demo — Day 1 Opening
### Wolf's Director Script — First 20 Minutes of Class

---

## What You're Engineering

Students have seen your ai-enterprise. They know something like Sophia exists.
The moment you pull out your phone and TEXT her live — with their real problem —
that's when the room changes.

This isn't a demo of what AI can do. It's proof of what YOU already do every day.
That authenticity is what no YouTube video can replicate.

**The jaw drop has two layers:**
1. She responded like a real EA — warm, specific, action-oriented
2. She already knows his calendar, his preferences, his business

The second layer hits about 10 seconds after the first. Watch for it on their faces.

---

## Setup (Night Before)

- [ ] Test Sophia with a clean prompt — make sure she's responsive
- [ ] Have your phone ready to AirPlay or screen mirror to the room display
- [ ] Know what's actually on your calendar that day — you'll use real data
- [ ] Ask one student before class to have a real work problem ready
      (maintenance issue, renewal coming up, owner complaint — anything real)
- [ ] Practice the debrief out loud once so it lands clean

---

## The Script

### Opening (2 min) — Don't rush this

Walk in. Don't start with slides. Don't introduce the agenda.

Start with:

> "Before we do anything else — I want to show you something.
>
> This is how my Tuesday started this morning."

Pull out your phone. Mirror it to the screen.

> "Her name is Sophia. She's my executive assistant.
> I interact with her the same way I'm texting you right now."

---

### The Live Text (3 min)

Ask the room:

> "Who's got something real on their plate right now?
> A tenant issue, a renewal, an owner who won't stop calling —
> give me something you actually need to deal with today."

Take their answer. Text it to Sophia live, in your own words, naturally.

**Example if no one volunteers:**

Text Sophia:
> "What does my day look like today and what's the most important thing I should tackle first?"

**Or — stronger for a PM audience:**

> "I've got a lease renewal coming up on [PROPERTY] and the tenant hasn't responded.
> What should I do and draft me something I can send them right now."

---

### Let It Land (60 seconds)

When Sophia responds — **say nothing.**

Let the room read it. Don't narrate. Don't explain.
The silence is part of it.

If Sophia asks a follow-up question, answer it naturally, like you're just texting.
Let the conversation breathe. That's the point.

---

### The Debrief (5 min)

After the response lands, put your phone down and ask:

> "What did you just see?"

Let them answer. They'll say things like:
- "She knew your calendar"
- "She wrote the email"
- "She asked a follow-up"
- "It felt like a real person"

Affirm each one. Then say:

> "What you didn't see is what makes this actually powerful.
>
> Sophia remembers. Every preference I've ever told her — no back-to-back meetings,
> 30-minute buffers, which owners are difficult, how I like my emails to sound —
> she has all of it. Every conversation makes her better at her job.
>
> She's not a chatbot. She doesn't just answer questions.
> She manages things. She pushes back on people trying to book me incorrectly.
> She drafts, schedules, reminds, and follows up — before I ask.
>
> That's an agent."

Pause. Then:

> "Now. You're not building Sophia today.
>
> Sophia took time. But here's what I know:
> every single piece of her started exactly where you're starting right now —
> a server, an API key, and a CLAUDE.md file.
>
> By end of today you have that foundation.
> By end of tomorrow you have a working agent of your own.
> By end of this class you have a roadmap to get from what you built
> to what you just saw.
>
> That's the 2.5 days. Let's get started."

---

### The Callback (use throughout the class)

**Day 1, when their server is running:**
> "You now have the same infrastructure Sophia runs on. That's not nothing."

**Day 2, when their agent produces its first output:**
> "That's the same feeling I had the first time Sophia worked.
> One thing. Running on its own. That's the beginning."

**Day 3, closing:**
> "You came in this morning and watched Sophia handle a real problem in 60 seconds.
> You're leaving with something running. And a path to the rest.
> The only question is how fast you build."

---

## What Makes This Different From Every Other AI Demo They've Seen

| Generic AI demo | This demo |
|---|---|
| Pre-scripted prompt | Real problem from the room |
| Presenter narrates | Silence while it loads |
| "Here's what AI can do" | "Here's what I do every morning" |
| Abstract capability | Wolf's actual calendar |
| Hopeful | Inevitable |

The last row is the most important. When they watch you text Sophia the same way
you'd text your assistant, it stops feeling like technology and starts feeling
like something they could actually have.

That's when they take out their phones to text their PM friends.
